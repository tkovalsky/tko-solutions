# Operational Recovery Assessment — Sales Playbook

**Purpose:** The end-to-end sales-and-delivery motion for **Offer 0 — Operational Recovery
Assessment**, exactly as defined in [`CURRENT_REALITY.md`](../../../CURRENT_REALITY.md):
**1 week, $5K–$8K**, the low-friction entry point whose purpose is to *convert into
diagnostics and builds.*

**This document does not create a new offer.** It operationalizes the existing Offer 0 into
a repeatable revenue motion. The expansion path is fixed by `CURRENT_REALITY.md`:

```
Assessment → Diagnostic → Build Sprint → Advisory
(Offer 0)    (Offer 1)    (Offer 2)       (Offer 3)
```

**Success-metric alignment:** Per `CURRENT_REALITY.md`, the relevant milestone is **Stage 1
— first paid assessment.** This playbook is the instrument for hitting it.

---

## Target Buyer

The Operational Recovery Assessment is sold to a leader who personally feels the pain of an
operation that depends on people instead of systems.

**Primary titles:**
- COO / VP Operations of a mid-market firm with data-rich, decision-poor workflows
- VP/Head of Revenue or Customer Success at a scaling company ("accounts churn while the CRM is fully updated")
- Director/VP of a healthcare operations function (prior auth, UM, care management) — **warm/referral only** until a provable healthcare engagement exists
- Operating partner at a PE firm (portfolio value creation — "reporting from every company, still can't see who's slipping")
- Owner/operator of a relationship-driven small business (advisory, brokerage, agency, professional services) — the near-exact RachelOS mirror

**Disqualifiers (do not sell the assessment to):**
- Buyers who want commodity tool-wiring / RPA at the lowest price
- Buyers who want a model-selection or "which AI" project
- Buyers requiring large staff-augmentation
- Anyone who needs a platform/SaaS commitment

**The qualifying belief:** the buyer suspects that *a person is the operating system* and
that more tools have not fixed it.

---

## Trigger Events

Reasons this buyer becomes reachable *now*:

- A key person left, is leaving, or is overloaded — and the operation visibly wobbled
- A failed or stalled AI pilot ("the model worked, nothing changed")
- A modernization/transformation program reporting progress without operational improvement
- An audit, compliance, or visibility concern exposed that "green" wasn't true
- A near-miss: a high-value relationship/account/case fell through the cracks
- New executive sponsor inheriting an operation they can't yet see into
- Cost pressure forcing a "do more with the same people" mandate
- Vendor or offshore delivery underperforming on ambiguous work

Each trigger maps to a [Transformation Story](../../proof/stories/TRANSFORMATION_STORY_LIBRARY.md)
(Human API, Dashboard Was Green, CRM Knew Everything, Workflow Bottleneck, AI Pilot
Failure) — use the matching story as the opening frame.

## Discovery Questions

Used in the first call to qualify and to locate the human-API dependency. Grouped by intent.

**Dependency / human-API:**
1. If your most knowledgeable operator were unreachable for two weeks, what would break first?
2. Who is the person everyone routes questions through, and what do they hold in their head?
3. Where does institutional knowledge live today — in a system, or in people?

**Record vs. action:**
4. Can your team answer "who needs attention today, and why?" without a meeting?
5. You have the data — where does it fail to become a decision or an action?
6. What slips through the cracks even though it's "in the system"?

**Visibility / truth:**
7. Where does reported status diverge from operational reality?
8. What do you find out later than you'd like, and why?

**AI / governance:**
9. Have you tried AI in this workflow? What happened after the demo?
10. Where would you *not* trust AI to act without a human approving first?

**Stakes / readiness:**
11. What does this cost you today — in risk, rework, or missed value? (qualitative is fine)
12. If we found the three places work stalls in one week, who would act on it?

## Assessment Workflow (1 week)

A fixed, repeatable 5-day motion sized to the $5K–$8K / 1-week envelope.

- **Day 0 — Scope & access:** confirm the single workflow/operation in scope; secure
  read-only access to relevant systems/docs; schedule 3–5 stakeholder conversations.
- **Days 1–2 — Signal gathering:** stakeholder interviews (30–45 min each); walk the target
  workflow end-to-end; collect artifacts (process docs, dashboards, queues, exception logs).
- **Day 3 — Analysis:** produce the four core analyses —
  - **Workflow map** (current-state, where work actually flows)
  - **Bottleneck analysis** (where work stalls and why)
  - **Dependency analysis** (where the human-API risk concentrates)
  - **AI readiness assessment** (where AI could safely reduce burden, and where it can't yet)
  plus an **operational risk assessment** (what breaks if a key person is unavailable).
- **Day 4 — Synthesis:** assemble the executive briefing; identify the top 3 stall points
  and the single highest-leverage next move; draft the expansion recommendation.
- **Day 5 — Readout:** deliver the executive briefing live; align on next step.

## Deliverables

Exactly the Offer 0 deliverables in `CURRENT_REALITY.md`:

1. **Workflow map** — current-state map of the target operation
2. **Bottleneck analysis** — where work stalls and the cost of the stall (qualitative)
3. **Dependency analysis** — where the operation depends on specific individuals (human-API risk)
4. **AI readiness assessment** — where AI could safely help, gated by human approval; where it isn't ready
5. **Operational risk assessment** — what degrades if a key person becomes unavailable
6. **Executive briefing** — the synthesized findings + recommended next step

**Format:** one concise briefing document + a live readout. No fabricated metrics; findings
are evidence-based and qualitative where numbers aren't verifiable.

---

## Executive Briefing Structure

The readout document/deck follows a fixed order so it's repeatable and converts:

1. **What we looked at** — scope, stakeholders, workflow in one slide
2. **What we found** — top 3 places work stalls, stated plainly
3. **The dependency** — where the operation runs through a person, not a system (the human-API finding)
4. **The risk** — what happens when that dependency is unavailable
5. **The AI reality** — where AI can safely help (human-approved) and where it can't yet
6. **The highest-leverage move** — the single most valuable next action
7. **The path** — how a Diagnostic (Offer 1) or Build Sprint (Offer 2) would address it
8. **Proof** — RachelOS as the live example of the system being recommended

Pair the readout with relevant
[RachelOS evidence](../../proof/rachelos/RACHELOS_EVIDENCE_LIBRARY.md) and the matching
[story](../../proof/stories/TRANSFORMATION_STORY_LIBRARY.md).

## Follow-Up Process

- **Within 24h of readout:** send the finalized briefing + a one-paragraph recommended next
  step (Diagnostic or Build), with scope and price range from `CURRENT_REALITY.md`.
- **Day 3:** check-in — answer questions, offer to present findings to additional stakeholders.
- **Day 7:** send the relevant proof asset (RachelOS evidence / a transformation story / the
  framework spine graphic) tied to their specific finding.
- **Day 14:** decision conversation — propose the Diagnostic or Build Sprint with a start date.
- **If no immediate yes:** move to a low-frequency nurture; the assessment finding is a
  durable reason to re-engage on the next trigger event.

## Expansion Path

The assessment is intentionally a wedge. Its job is to earn the right to the next offer.

| From | To | Trigger to advance | Price (per CURRENT_REALITY.md) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Assessment** (Offer 0) | **Diagnostic** (Offer 1) | Buyer wants the full operating-model + 90-day roadmap | $15K–$30K, 2–3 wks |
| **Diagnostic** (Offer 1) | **Build Sprint** (Offer 2) | Buyer wants the decision layer built, not just mapped | $45K–$150K, 6–12 wks |
| **Build Sprint** (Offer 2) | **Advisory** (Offer 3) | Buyer needs governance so the system doesn't drift | $12K–$25K/mo, 3–12 mo |

**Conversion principle:** every assessment finding should name a specific, scoped problem
that the next offer solves — so the upsell is the buyer's own conclusion, not a pitch.

---

## What this playbook must not do

Per mission constraints and `CURRENT_REALITY.md`:
- Do **not** introduce new offers or change pricing.
- Do **not** promise a software platform — the assessment sells judgment + a path, with
  RachelOS as live proof.
- Do **not** attach metrics that aren't verifiable.
- Do **not** lead healthcare prospects with unprovable case studies — use advisory framing
  and warm/referral entry only.
