## Executive Summary

TKO Solutions exists to solve a recurring organizational failure mode:

**Critical operational knowledge lives inside people instead of systems.**

Organizations often have:

- Data
- Dashboards
- Reports
- CRMs
- Workflows
- Documentation
- Project plans

Yet critical work still depends on a small number of individuals who serve as:

- Human APIs
- Institutional memory
- Escalation paths
- Decision engines
- Workflow translators
- Operational glue

When those people become unavailable, overloaded, or leave, execution degrades rapidly.

TKO designs and builds **Operational Knowledge Systems** that transform fragmented
information and institutional knowledge into governed, actionable operating systems.

The current public-site buyer lens is:

- Company identity: Operational Knowledge Systems for complex workflows
- Primary market: healthcare workflow modernization
- Primary offer: Operational Recovery Assessment ($5K-$8K)
- Primary proof: RachelOS
- Primary credibility: healthcare transformation experience
- Target buyer: Director+ healthcare operations, transformation, product, implementation,
  customer success, consulting partner, or operational leader
- Active administrative burden, workflow modernization, transformation recovery, or AI
  adoption pressure
- Dependence on key people who function as human APIs
- Need for a senior operator-builder who can diagnose the operating truth before larger
  workflow or AI spend is committed

## Core Thesis

The problem is not lack of information.

The problem is that information does not become operational judgment.

Most organizations have:

- Information ≠ Understanding
- Understanding ≠ Decision
- Decision ≠ Execution

TKO focuses on the layers between information and execution.

## Pattern Observed Across All Engagements

### Healthcare Programs

**Symptoms:**

- One program manager knows everything
- Critical dependencies are undocumented
- Teams operate from different realities
- Status appears healthy while risk accumulates
- Escalations depend on specific individuals

**Result:** The person becomes the operating system.

### RachelOS

**Symptoms:**

- CRM contains information
- Notes exist
- Emails exist
- Texts exist
- Guide downloads exist

Yet only Rachel knows:

- who matters
- why they matter
- what happened previously
- what question to ask
- what content to send
- when to follow up

**Result:** Rachel becomes the operating system.

### CRE Intelligence

**Symptoms:**

- Market knowledge exists
- Observations exist
- Property information exists

But intelligence remains fragmented and non-reusable.

**Result:** The analyst becomes the operating system.

### Unified Pattern

The recurring human bottleneck:

```
Human → Knowledge → Judgment → Action
```

TKO converts this into a governed system:

```
Signals → Memory → Facts → State → Priority → Recommendation → Human Approval → Action → Outcome
```

### Tier-1 Opportunity — Prior Authorization Operational Assessment

Prior Authorization Operational Assessment is a Tier-1 TKO opportunity because it sits directly at the intersection of Human APIs, Administrative Burden, Healthcare Transformation, AI Adoption, and Operational Assessments.

TKO should not initially pursue Gold Card platforms, prior authorization automation products, payer technology products, FHIR infrastructure products, or claims adjudication platforms. Those are large-market product categories with significant implementation complexity and long sales cycles.

TKO's wedge is operational quality improvement.

Core GTM insight:

> Gold Card eligibility is not the problem. Operational quality is the problem.

Practice administrators do not wake up asking for Gold Card programs, FHIR integrations, or prior authorization platforms. They ask why requests are denied, why authorization takes so long, why staff spend hours on manual work, why some providers are approved while others are not, why certain payers create constant friction, and why work depends on specific people.

The assessment should focus on administrative burden, denial reduction, workflow quality, operational consistency, and human dependency risk. Gold Card readiness is an output of operational quality; it is not the product.

Canonical strategy note: [Prior Authorization Operational Assessment](docs/strategy/PRIOR_AUTHORIZATION_OPERATIONAL_ASSESSMENT.md).

### Secondary Position — Operational Knowledge Systems

Systems that capture institutional knowledge, operational context, workflow state, and
decision logic to reduce dependence on individual people.

### Internal Category — Operational Intelligence

Use internally. Do not lead externally with the term.

Lead externally with:

- Workflow Modernization
- Administrative Burden Reduction
- AI Adoption
- Operational Visibility
- Transformation Recovery

### Diagnostic Lens — Human API Dependency (a finding type, not an offer)

"Human API" is a **diagnostic lens and finding type**, not a product, offer, separate
assessment, or pricing tier. It names a person who has become the undocumented integration
layer between teams, systems, decisions, approvals, or workflows — for example, the one
manager everyone calls because no one knows how a process actually works, the analyst who
manually reconciles data between systems, the operations lead who owns all the exceptions,
or the PA specialist who knows payer-specific rules that exist nowhere else.

Treat Human API dependency as one possible **output** of an assessment — a dependency
pattern, risk indicator, and knowledge-concentration signal — surfaced alongside the other
evaluation dimensions below. It never becomes the thing the customer buys. The customer buys
the **Operational Recovery Assessment** (and, in healthcare, the **Prior Authorization
Operational Assessment**).

## Service Architecture

- **Price:** $5K–$8K

- **Deliverables:** workflow map · bottleneck analysis · dependency analysis · AI readiness assessment · operational risk assessment · executive briefing

### Offer 0A — Prior Authorization Operational Assessment

- **Status:** Tier-1 healthcare assessment wedge.
- **Inputs:** PA submissions · denial reasons · specialty · payer mix · workflow documentation · staffing model.
- **Outputs:** administrative burden analysis · denial analysis · exception analysis · Human API risk analysis · Gold Card readiness assessment.
- **Purpose:** Diagnose operational quality before automation, AI, Gold Card, FHIR, or platform work.
- **Constraint:** Assessment only. Not a Gold Card platform, prior authorization platform, claims platform, FHIR product, or payer technology product.

- **Price:** $15K–$30K

- **Price:** $45K–$150K
- **Deliverables:** operational memory model · fact model · state model · prioritization model · workflow orchestration · human approval model · operational dashboard · implementation artifacts

## Strategic Constraint

For the next 90 days, the GTM business optimizes for proof and revenue:

- Build proof. Not client-facing software or platforms.
- Publish evidence. Not frameworks.
- Sell assessments. Not platforms.

"Not software" means TKO does not build or sell a **client-facing** platform or SaaS product.
It does **not** prohibit the internal TIF tooling above: TIF is the sanctioned engineering path
precisely because it *produces* the proof, content, and assessment assets this constraint
demands. The consulting capability is already demonstrated (RachelOS); the market now needs to
see it.
