---

## Page Job

The Diagnostic page must justify a $25k-$50k Operational Intelligence Diagnostic by making the buyer understand what will be diagnosed, what evidence will be produced, what decisions the engagement enables, and why a fixed-scope diagnostic is the correct entry point.

Primary conversion action: Start the Diagnostic.

The page should qualify buyers as much as it persuades them. The right buyer already knows work is failing or stalled; they need operational truth, prioritization, and a credible next step.

---

## Section 1: Hero / Diagnostic Position

### Purpose

Make the paid diagnostic concrete and serious.

### Content Architecture

- Identify the diagnostic as the entry point into TKO's service ladder.
- State that it is designed for leaders who cannot see where complex work is actually failing.
- Position the diagnostic as evidence generation, not discovery, coaching, or consultation.
- Healthcare examples should appear first.

### Proof Required

- Operational Truth Framework.
- Enterprise healthcare transformation credibility.
- Production Operational Intelligence System proof.

### CTA Flow

Primary CTA above the fold: Start the Diagnostic.

No secondary CTA.

## Section 2: Who It Is For

### Purpose

Help qualified buyers self-identify quickly.

### Included Buyers

- Healthcare operations leaders dealing with invisible workflow failure.
- Transformation leaders responsible for stalled modernization or AI programs.
- PMO and program leaders managing cross-team dependency risk.
- COOs and VPs of Operations who need a trustworthy view of operational failure points.
- CIOs, CDOs, and digital leaders whose AI or workflow investments are not being adopted.
- Health-tech operations leaders whose customer implementations, follow-up, or account workflows are at risk.
- Secondary regulated operations leaders in Financial Services, Wealth Management, and Capital Markets with similar workflow visibility failures.

### Situations

- A transformation program is active but not producing measurable operational progress.
- AI pilots or automation efforts have not reached trusted production use.
- Administrative burden is growing despite technology investment.
- Work crosses teams and systems but no one can see where it stalls.
- Governance meetings happen, but decisions and accountability remain unclear.
- A new leader inherited a program and needs operational truth fast.
- A sponsor needs a credible 90-day recovery roadmap.

### CTA Placement

No CTA required inside this section unless long-page pacing requires one.

## Section 3: Who It Is Not For

### Purpose

Protect positioning and filter poor-fit demand.

### Excluded Buyers

- Buyers looking for AI tool recommendations, prompt engineering, or AI trend advice.
- Teams seeking generic PMO support, meeting facilitation, or staff augmentation.
- Organizations that need to be convinced a problem exists.
- Teams seeking a low-cost audit, exploratory workshop, or informal consultation.
- Buyers seeking full software development as the first step.
- Teams unwilling to provide stakeholder access, workflow artifacts, or operational evidence.
- Organizations looking for autonomous AI decisions without human approval and governance.

### Positioning Logic

The Diagnostic is for an urgent, funded operating problem. It is not a broad strategy exercise or a way to outsource internal alignment before leadership accepts that operational failure exists.

### CTA Placement

No CTA. This section is intentionally disqualifying.

## Section 4: What The Diagnostic Examines

### Purpose

Define scope and make the work feel rigorous enough to support premium pricing.

### Content Architecture

Use the Operational Truth Framework as the spine:

1. What is supposed to happen?
2. What actually happens?
3. Where does work stall?
4. What is known but unused?
5. What should the next action be?
6. Where can AI help without taking control?
7. How is success measured?

### Healthcare Examples

- Prior authorization review paths.
- Care management handoffs.
- Provider operations workflows.
- Administrative burden and manual review.
- Interoperability-driven operating changes.
- AI-enabled decision support adoption.

### Secondary Examples

- Wealth management platform handoffs.
- Trade support and settlement workflow.
- Portfolio operations visibility.
- Loan administration queues.

### Proof Required

- Explain that the diagnostic is limited to the workflows or program areas where operational truth can be established.
- Avoid claiming broad enterprise transformation inside a three-week diagnostic.

### CTA Placement

CTA after the framework explanation: Start the Diagnostic.

## Section 5: Deliverables

### Purpose

Make the buyer understand exactly what they receive.

### Required Deliverables

1. Operational Truth Assessment: supposed-to-happen versus actually-happens view of the target workflow or program area.
2. Failure-Point and Manual-Handoff Inventory: stalls, manual workarounds, invisible handoffs, and avoidable rework.
3. Visibility Gap Map: where leadership cannot see work, ownership, state, risk, or next action.
4. Administrative Burden and Leakage Inventory: where time, cost, delay, or rework exceeds what the workflow requires.
5. AI Opportunity and Control Model: where AI can help, where humans must approve, and where automation would create risk.
6. Top 10 Recovery Opportunities: ranked by operational value, implementation risk, and effort.
7. 90-Day Recovery Roadmap: sequenced actions with owners, gates, and success criteria.
8. Executive Readout: 60-minute readout for the sponsor and relevant leaders.
9. Follow-Up Q&A: 30-day window for clarification on findings.

### Proof Required

- Show these deliverables as decision assets, not documentation.
- Reinforce that each deliverable helps leadership decide what to fix, build, govern, or stop.

### CTA Placement

CTA after deliverables: Start the Diagnostic.

## Section 6: Timeline

### Purpose

Communicate scope control and buying confidence.

### Architecture

Week 1: Stakeholder interviews and artifact review.

Week 2: Analysis, failure mapping, visibility-gap mapping, and opportunity ranking.

Week 3: Roadmap development, readout preparation, and executive readout.

### Scope Boundaries

- Maximum 6-8 stakeholder interviews.
- Maximum 1-2 workflow or program areas.
- Requires access to existing workflow documentation, process artifacts, reporting, or operational evidence.
- Scope adjustment required if stakeholder count, workflow count, or technical complexity exceeds the diagnostic frame.

### CTA Placement

No CTA required unless this section appears late on the page.

---

## Section 7: Process

### Purpose

Show how TKO works without turning the page into a methodology whitepaper.

### Process Steps

1. Intake and scope confirmation: confirm the operating problem, decision needed, systems/workflows involved, and sponsor.
2. Evidence review: inspect artifacts, workflow documents, reporting, dashboards, backlogs, operating cadences, or system outputs.
3. Stakeholder interviews: interview leaders and operators close to the failure points.
4. Operational truth mapping: compare intended workflow to actual workflow.
5. Failure and visibility analysis: identify stalls, gaps, handoffs, decision latency, and governance breakdowns.
6. Opportunity ranking: prioritize recovery opportunities by value, risk, and effort.
7. Executive readout: present findings and 90-day recovery roadmap.

### Proof Required

- Make the process feel evidence-led and constrained.
- Do not describe open-ended consulting activities.

### CTA Placement

CTA after process if buyer has enough confidence to proceed.

## Section 8: Outcomes

### Purpose

Translate deliverables into executive value.

### Outcome Architecture

After the Diagnostic, the buyer should have:

- A credible view of where work is actually failing.
- A shared fact base across operations, transformation, technology, and governance stakeholders.
- A ranked list of recovery opportunities.
- A 90-day operating roadmap.
- A decision on whether to build an Operational Intelligence System, adjust governance, redesign workflow, or stop low-value work.
- A clearer AI control model where AI is relevant.

### Non-Outcomes

The Diagnostic does not implement recommendations, replace systems, deploy AI tooling, or provide ongoing advisory support. Those belong to downstream engagements.

### CTA Placement

CTA after outcomes: Start the Diagnostic.

## Section 9: Pricing Position

### Purpose

Justify premium pricing and filter low-fit inquiries.

### Positioning

The Diagnostic should be positioned as a fixed-scope executive operating assessment for urgent, funded problems. The pricing range supports serious stakeholder access, rigorous analysis, and executive-ready decision assets.

### Pricing Architecture

- Standard position: $25k fixed diagnostic.
- Upper range: up to $50k when workflow complexity, stakeholder access, evidence review, or enterprise scope expands.
- Payment terms may follow 50% at start and 50% at readout.
- No pricing below the established floor.

### Value Logic

The Diagnostic is justified when the cost of invisible failure is materially higher than the diagnostic fee:

- Delayed transformation outcomes.
- Manual review burden.
- Program slippage.
- Failed AI adoption.
- Governance drag.
- Operational rework.
- Customer or member experience risk.

### CTA Placement

CTA after pricing: Start the Diagnostic.

---

## Section 10: Follow-On Path

### Purpose

Clarify that the Diagnostic is the entry point, not the whole service ladder.

### Architecture

After the Diagnostic, three outcomes are possible:

1. Internal execution: the client uses the roadmap independently.
2. Operating System Build: TKO designs and helps implement the system identified by the Diagnostic.
3. Fractional Operating System Advisor: TKO supports governance, cadence, and momentum after diagnostic or build.

### Guardrails

- Build and Fractional are downstream paths.
- Do not make the page feel like the Diagnostic is a thinly veiled sales pitch.
- The Diagnostic must stand alone as valuable.

### CTA Placement

No separate CTA for Build or Fractional. Repeat Start the Diagnostic only.

## Section 11: FAQ

### Purpose

Remove buying friction without weakening the offer.

### FAQ Architecture

Recommended questions:

1. What makes this different from a consulting assessment?
2. What makes this different from PMO or program review?
3. What makes this different from BI or dashboard work?
4. Do we need clean data before starting?
5. Do we need an AI initiative for this to be relevant?
6. Can this apply outside healthcare?
7. Who needs to participate?
8. What artifacts do you review?
9. What happens if the problem is bigger than the diagnostic scope?
10. What do we receive at the end?
11. Can TKO implement the recommendations?
12. Why is the diagnostic priced at this level?
13. What should we not expect from the diagnostic?

### Answer Strategy

Answers should be direct, scope-conscious, and diagnostic-led. Avoid defensive explanations, generic consulting language, or claims that imply guaranteed downstream savings.

### CTA Placement

CTA after FAQ: Start the Diagnostic.

Convert qualified buyers who understand fit, scope, price, and output.

Destination: diagnostic intake.

Intake should collect:

- Systems or workflows involved.
- What is not working.
