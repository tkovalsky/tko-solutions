# Authority Content Strategy

Status: Phase 2 build asset
Source of truth: `docs/TKO_ARCHITECTURE_DECISIONS.md`, `docs/WEBSITE_ARCHITECTURE.md`
Purpose: Define TKO's authority content strategy around executive operating pain and the Operational Intelligence category.

---

## Strategy

TKO authority content should help executive and senior operational buyers recognize hidden operating failure: stalled transformation, invisible workflows, governance breakdown, decision latency, cross-team coordination failure, AI adoption failure, and enterprise value risk created by founder/key-person dependency.

The content strategy must not become generic AI content. TKO should not publish AI trends, AI tools roundups, prompt engineering advice, ChatGPT tutorials, or thought leadership that positions the firm as AI consulting.

The content should make one point repeatedly from different operating angles: organizations cannot see where work is actually failing.

For healthcare, Prior Authorization Operational Assessment is a Tier-1 opportunity and should be treated as a concrete content wedge. The argument is operational, not product-led: Gold Card eligibility is an output of operational quality; it is not the product.

## Editorial Standards

Every authority article should:

- Begin from an executive operating pain, not a trend.
- Name the invisible failure beneath the symptom.
- Use healthcare examples first when practical.
- Tie the issue back to Operational Intelligence.
- Explain what leadership needs to see, decide, govern, or measure.
- Route to the Diagnostic when the reader has an active problem.

Every authority article should avoid:

- Generic AI trend commentary.
- Tool comparisons.
- Prompt engineering.
- AI productivity tips.
- Gold Card platform positioning.
- Prior authorization platform positioning.
- FHIR or claims-integration product framing as the initial wedge.
- Generic digital transformation advice.
- PMO templates as the core artifact.
- Unsupported claims of client outcomes.
- New offers, new ICPs, or alternate positioning.

## Content Formats

### Primary Formats

- Executive problem articles.
- Framework explainers.
- Case-study pattern breakdowns.
- Diagnostic readiness articles.
- Operating model breakdowns.

### Secondary Formats

- LinkedIn posts derived from articles.
- One-page proof summaries.
- Referral-support content.
- Case study index and cards.

### Deferred Formats

- Gated guides.
- Long-form reports.
- Webinars.
- Industry landing pages outside healthcare.
- SEO-heavy content hubs.

Deferred formats should not lead the first 90 days unless the site has sufficient proof and content depth.

---

## CTA Rules

Primary CTA destination: Diagnostic page or contact intake, depending on reader readiness.

Use this routing:

- Articles explaining a problem -> Diagnostic page.
- Articles explaining TKO method -> Diagnostic page.
- Articles proving system capability -> relevant case study, then Diagnostic page.
- Articles aimed at conversion-ready buyers -> contact / diagnostic intake.

Do not use newsletter signup, download guide, book a free consultation, or tool-demo CTAs as the primary action.

---

## Proof Integration

Authority content should use proof conservatively:

- RachelDelray proves production system pattern and human-controlled operational intelligence.
- Healthcare prior authorization, care management, and interoperability examples prove enterprise complexity and transformation pattern recognition.
- Financial Services examples support cross-industry pattern recognition.
- Metrics must follow attribution rules.
- Evidence gaps should be respected rather than filled with speculative claims.

---

## First 90-Day Editorial Priorities

Priority order:

1. Workflow visibility failure.
2. Transformation recovery.
3. Operational Intelligence vs BI/PMO/AI tools.
4. Governance breakdown.
5. AI adoption failure through workflow/control lens.
6. Healthcare operations examples.
7. Prior Authorization Operational Assessment articles.
8. Financial Services support pieces.
9. Enterprise value and exit readiness as the valuation consequence of Human API dependency and operational opacity.

The first 90 days should build enough authority for warm leads, referral partners, and skeptical buyers to validate TKO before starting a diagnostic conversation.
