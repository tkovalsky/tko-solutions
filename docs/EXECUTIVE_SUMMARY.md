## Core Narrative Assessment

The proposed narrative holds:

This is supported by every major document and by the working code in RachelDelray.com. **The risk is not in the narrative. It is in the evidence gaps and the public-facing contradictions.**

## Biggest Positioning Risk

**The live website destroys credibility with every warm lead who visits it.**

The current site says "no-code/AI prototypes · $750 diagnostic · $3k–$5k sprint." A healthcare VP who receives a thoughtful outreach message about operational transformation recovery, visits the site, and sees that copy will not book a call. They will not respond. They will assume the outreach was a mistake.

This is not a visual design problem. It is a positioning contradiction that exists on the public internet right now.

**Secondary risk:** The pricing floor is not decided. Selling at $5k–$15k and selling at $25k require different buyers, different networks, and different authority levels to approve the purchase. Running both simultaneously confuses every conversation.

## Tier-1 Healthcare Assessment Wedge

Prior Authorization Operational Assessment should be treated as a Tier-1 TKO opportunity because it sits at the intersection of Human APIs, Administrative Burden, Healthcare Transformation, AI Adoption, and Operational Assessments.

The GTM insight is: Gold Card eligibility is not the problem; operational quality is the problem. Practice administrators ask why authorizations are denied, why work takes so long, why manual burden persists, why payer friction repeats, and why the process depends on specific people.

Do not market Gold Card platforms, PA platforms, claims platforms, payer technology products, FHIR infrastructure, or AI platforms as the initial wedge.
