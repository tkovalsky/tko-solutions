## 1. What business is TKO actually in?

**Today:** TKO is a solo consulting practice with zero paying clients, one impressive proof-of-pattern build, and a strong portfolio of employer-era healthcare transformation experience.

**The aspiration:** A business operating systems consultancy that helps organizations replace fragmented workflows with governed, AI-assisted decision systems.

**The honest gap:** The aspiration is correct and worth pursuing. But it is not the current reality. The category is earned through delivered engagements — not through documentation, positioning work, or a well-built side project. Until TKO has one paid engagement completed and one case study published, the business is in the pre-revenue stage, not the "consultancy" stage.

**The practical business TKO is in right now:** A healthcare operational transformation advisor with strong credentials who is packaging his experience into a fixed-scope consulting offer while employed full-time. That is not a criticism. That is the honest starting position, and it is a viable one. Pretending to be further along is the mistake.

**What this means operationally:** Don't sell the category. Sell the specific offer. "We are a business operating systems consultancy" is a long-term brand claim. "I help healthcare organizations find where their transformation is stalling — $25k, 3 weeks, 10 prioritized fixes, 90-day roadmap" is a purchasable offer. Lead with the offer. Earn the category.

## 2. What should TKO sell first?

**The Operational Intelligence Diagnostic. $25k. 3 weeks. Network-sold.**

This is the right first offer because:
- It requires only one buyer to approve it (no committee, no procurement cycle for most healthcare organizations)
- It does not require a completed website, a published case study, or a LinkedIn following
- It produces the proof asset (a completed diagnostic) that makes every subsequent offer easier to sell
- It establishes a pricing floor ($25k) that signals enterprise positioning
- It can be delivered in 3 weeks with 15–20 hours of focused work — feasible with employment constraints
- It creates a natural expansion path (diagnostic → build → advisory)

**How to sell it:** Network-first. Not cold outbound. Not LinkedIn campaigns. Not the website. The first engagement will come from a former colleague who knows the founder's work, a consulting partner who needs specialist delivery support, or a warm introduction from someone who does. Start there. Don't wait for inbound.

**The pitch:** "I'm running a fixed-scope operational diagnostic for healthcare leaders dealing with stalled transformations, AI that isn't producing results, or administrative burden that keeps growing despite investment. Three weeks, $25k, you get the ten highest-value recovery opportunities ranked by effort and impact plus a 90-day roadmap. Do you know one person I should talk to?"

## 4. What can realistically be sold in the next 30 days?

**Realistic:** One diagnostic conversation. Possibly one proposal. Possibly one signed engagement.

**Requirements for a signed engagement in 30 days:**
1. The website must not undermine the pitch (fix the $750 copy first)
2. A one-page diagnostic offer sheet must exist (1–2 hours to create)
3. At least one warm network conversation must happen — a former colleague who knows the founder's work and either has a buyer problem or knows someone who does
4. The founder must be willing to make the ask directly and early

**What is not required for a signed engagement in 30 days:**
- A published case study
- A LinkedIn post
- A redesigned website
- Authority PDFs
- A thought leadership archive

The first engagement will be sold on the strength of personal credibility, a clear articulation of the buyer's problem, and a specific offer. The content marketing machine comes after that.

**The realistic risk:** The founder's network may not contain an immediately qualified buyer. In that case, the 30-day outcome is not a signed engagement — it is 10–15 conversations that surface who the warmest leads are, what language resonates, and which problem description opens doors. That is still progress.

**What would prevent a signed engagement in 30 days:**
- Continuing to build documentation, frameworks, and positioning materials instead of making outreach calls
- Waiting for the website, LinkedIn, or case studies to be perfect before reaching out
- Pricing the diagnostic below $20k in response to buyer hesitation (this signals lack of confidence, not accommodation)
- Failing to make a direct ask

## 6. What is the clearest path from side consulting to a standalone business?

**The honest answer:** It is longer and harder than the roadmap implies.

`TKO_ROADMAP.md` targets "$10k+/month outside employment by August–September 2026" and full employment replacement by September. This assumes a first diagnostic is signed in June–July 2026, expands to a second engagement, and generates enough pipeline to justify leaving Cognizant. That sequence is possible but not guaranteed, and it requires starting outreach conversations immediately — not after the website, LinkedIn, and case studies are done.

**The viable path, honestly described:**

**Months 1–3:** Sell and deliver one diagnostic. Generate one case study. Start 20+ network conversations. If the diagnostic converts to a build engagement ($50k–$75k), revenue trajectory becomes viable. If it doesn't convert, start the next diagnostic conversation immediately.

**Months 3–6:** Two engagements completed or in progress. Case study published. LinkedIn presence established. Inbound beginning to supplement outbound. Pipeline conversation is meaningful (5+ qualified opportunities).

**Months 6–12:** Three or more completed engagements. Recurring advisory revenue from at least one client. Case studies substantiated with measurable outcomes. A decision about employment replacement is now data-driven, not faith-based.

**The transition decision should be driven by pipeline, not by a date.** The right time to leave employment is when the pipeline contains enough qualified opportunities to make $10k+/month revenue probable within 60 days — not certain, but probable. Until that pipeline exists, employment provides the financial foundation that allows TKO to be selective about early clients rather than desperate.

**The most dangerous path:** Building the brand infrastructure (website, positioning docs, LinkedIn, frameworks, PDFs) while delaying the conversations that actually produce revenue. Documentation is not a precondition for outreach. Documentation is what you create to support conversations that are already happening. Start the conversations before the infrastructure is perfect.

**What this business looks like if it works at 12 months:**
- 4–6 completed engagements, 2–3 healthcare, 1–2 non-healthcare referrals
- $300k–$500k annualized revenue at full capacity
- 1–2 published named case studies
- A fractional advisory client providing recurring revenue
- A reputation in healthcare operational transformation that generates referrals without cold outreach

**What this business does not look like at 12 months:**
- A SaaS platform with multiple paying tenants
- A team of consultants
- A multi-vertical consulting firm
- An AI product company

The 12-month business is a highly credible solo consulting practice with a named methodology, real proof, and a small number of high-value clients. That is a real business, worth building. The platform, the team, and the expansion can follow once the pattern is proven and the revenue is real.
