# Phase 2 - Offer Design

## Offer 1: Diagnostic Offer

Name:

**Healthcare Transformation Recovery Diagnostic**

Alternate problem-led packaging:

**Administrative Burden Reduction Diagnostic**

Method:

**Powered by the Operational Truth Framework**

Buyer:

VP Ops, VP Utilization Management, VP Provider Ops, CIO, transformation sponsor, health-tech COO.

Problem:

The organization is spending too much, moving too slowly, or failing to realize transformation/AI value, but leadership cannot see where work actually stalls, what constraints are real, or which changes would improve cost, speed, visibility, accountability, adoption, and ROI fastest.

Deliverables:

- "What is supposed to happen vs. what actually happens" map
- Failure-point and manual-handoff inventory
- Operational visibility gap assessment
- Administrative burden and cost-leakage map
- AI opportunity map by operational constraint
- Human-approval and control model
- Top 10 burden-reduction / recovery opportunities ranked by value, risk, and effort
- 90-day executive roadmap
- Executive outcomes model: cost, risk, speed, visibility, accountability, adoption, ROI
- 60-minute executive readout

Timeline:

3 weeks.

Pricing:

**$25k fixed fee**

Optional range:

$20k-$35k depending on scope and access.

Why it sells:

It is concrete, low-risk, budgetable, and speaks to a problem executives already have.

## Offer 2: Sprint Offer

Name:

**Operational Intelligence Design Sprint**

Buyer:

CIO, Head of AI, VP Ops, product/engineering leader, health-tech founder.

Problem:

The organization has a high-friction operating area, but lacks the visibility, decision rules, measurement, and human/AI control model required to reduce burden or recover execution.

Deliverables:

- Target operating workflow design
- Operational signal and visibility model
- Decisioning model: what should be surfaced, escalated, automated, or approved
- AI role definition: extract, summarize, prioritize, draft, recommend, or act-with-approval
- Data and integration requirements
- Control and auditability requirements
- Exception handling model
- Build-ready user stories or implementation backlog
- Pilot success metrics

Timeline:

4 weeks.

Pricing:

**$35k-$50k fixed fee**

Why it sells:

It converts operational pain into a build-ready intelligence and decision system without making AI the headline.

## Offer 3: Transformation Offer

Name:

**Healthcare Transformation Recovery Engagement**

Buyer:

COO, CIO, Chief Transformation Officer, payer operations executive, consulting partner.

Problem:

A modernization or AI-enabled transformation program is already underway, but value is not materializing, milestones are slipping, costs are rising, and leaders do not have a trusted view of what is blocking execution.

Deliverables:

- Forensic operating review
- "What is true" executive brief
- Recovery backlog
- Governance and decision-rights model
- Delivery cadence and escalation model
- Workflow and operating-model redesign for 1-2 priority processes
- AI/control design where appropriate
- Weekly sponsor readout

Timeline:

8-10 weeks.

Pricing:

**$60k-$100k fixed fee**

Why it sells:

The buyer has active pain and budget. Todd's evidence is strongest when recovery, workflow, systems, and executive execution intersect.

## Offer 4: Fractional Offer

Name:

**Fractional Operational Transformation Advisor**

Buyer:

Mid-market healthcare organization, health-tech vendor, consulting firm, payer ops team.

Problem:

Leadership needs senior operational transformation leadership, but does not need or cannot hire a full-time operating partner.

Deliverables:

- Weekly transformation operating cadence
- Administrative burden reduction / transformation recovery backlog
- Executive status and risk reporting
- Product/engineering/ops alignment
- AI governance and human-approval guidance
- Vendor and stakeholder coordination
- Monthly operating-system review

Timeline:

3-6 months.

Pricing:

**$15k-$25k/month**

Why it sells:

Works after a diagnostic or sprint. Harder to sell cold because it can sound like staff augmentation unless packaged around outcomes.
