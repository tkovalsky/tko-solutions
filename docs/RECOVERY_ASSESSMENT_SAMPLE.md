# Sample Operational Recovery Assessment

**Purpose:** Show a prospective buyer what a focused Recovery Assessment delivers.

**Important:** This is a realistic anonymized example for format and decision quality. It is not a client case study, a measured customer result, or a promise that every engagement will produce the same findings.

## Example operating context

**Company:** Anonymized multi-location service business

**Business trigger:** Revenue opportunities and service requests are captured across a CRM, email, calls, and spreadsheets. Leadership sees strong activity but inconsistent follow-up, delayed decisions, and no trusted view of what requires attention.

**Sponsor decision:** Determine whether the business needs a new platform, a workflow redesign, or a focused operating layer before funding more technology.

---

# 1. Executive Summary

## Decision requested

Do not fund a replacement platform before correcting the operating constraint: critical context is fragmented, priority is manually reconstructed, and exceptions depend on a few experienced people.

## What we found

- The CRM records activity but does not establish a trusted current state or next action.
- The highest-value work is reconstructed during meetings and through direct messages to experienced staff.
- Different teams use different informal priority rules.
- Exceptions are escalated by familiarity rather than a documented decision path.
- Existing reporting describes volume and status, but does not identify which owner must make which decision next.

## Recommended next move

Run a 30-day workflow and decision-rights redesign focused on one high-value customer journey. Build only the minimum operating mechanisms needed to make context, priority, ownership, and escalation visible.

## Claim boundary

This example identifies a plausible constraint and a decision path. It does not estimate savings, revenue, capacity release, or implementation cost without actual operating evidence.

---

# 2. Current State

## Intended workflow

1. Inquiry or request enters the CRM.
2. Team qualifies and assigns an owner.
3. Owner follows up and advances the work.
4. Exceptions are escalated.
5. Leadership reviews performance.

## Actual workflow observed in this example

1. Requests arrive through CRM, email, calls, and spreadsheets.
2. Staff manually reconcile context before acting.
3. Priority depends on manager memory, urgency signals in messages, and ad hoc meetings.
4. Exceptions move to whichever experienced person is available.
5. Leadership receives activity reporting but must convene people to reconstruct risk and decide what matters.

## What changed because of the assessment

**Before:** The business described the issue as a need for a new platform.

**After:** The sponsor can see that the first constraint is fragmented operating context and implicit priority/exception handling.

**Evidence:** Interview themes, source inventory, workflow comparison, and decision register in this sample.

**What this does not prove:** That a platform replacement is unnecessary in every case, or that a redesign alone will produce a defined financial result.

# 4. Dependency Map

```text
Customer / opportunity signal
        ↓
CRM ── Email ── Calls ── Spreadsheet
        ↓
Manual context reconstruction by staff
        ↓
Informal priority and exception judgment
        ↓
Experienced manager becomes escalation point
        ↓
Leadership meeting to reconstruct risk and decide action
```

## Dependency findings

- The experienced manager is both escalation path and relationship-memory layer.
- No defined authority model resolves conflicting information between sources.
- Leadership's decision speed depends on a meeting rather than a durable operating view.
- A platform selection would not, by itself, resolve implicit priority and exception rules.

# 6. Recommendations

## 1. Establish an operating source-of-truth model

Define what system or person is authoritative for each critical fact, how conflicts are handled, and which facts require human confirmation.

## 2. Pilot a priority-and-exception operating layer

For one high-value workflow, create a visible current state, ranked work list, exception categories, and escalation path. Do not attempt enterprise-wide automation first.

## 3. Redesign the operating review

Replace a status-only meeting with a weekly review of top constraints, exposed work, decisions required, owners, missing evidence, and escalations.

## 4. Delay vendor selection until decision rights are explicit

Use the redesigned workflow to evaluate technology against the actual operating behavior required—not against generic feature lists.
