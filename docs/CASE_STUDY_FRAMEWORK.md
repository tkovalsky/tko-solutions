# Case Study Framework

Status: Phase 2 build asset
Source of truth: `docs/TKO_ARCHITECTURE_DECISIONS.md`, `docs/WEBSITE_ARCHITECTURE.md`, `docs/CASE_STUDIES_V2.md`
Purpose: Define the canonical structure, proof model, attribution rules, and anonymization rules for TKO case studies.

---

## Case Study Job

Every case study must prove a pattern: complex work was failing in ways leadership could not see, TKO identified the operational truth, and a system, model, workflow, or governance change made the failure visible and actionable.

Case studies should be organized by operating problem, not only by industry. Healthcare proof should dominate at launch. Financial Services, Wealth Management, Capital Markets, and enterprise operations can support the pattern when evidence is available.

---

## Canonical Case Study Template

Every case study must follow this structure in this order.

### 1. Situation

Purpose: Establish the operating environment, business context, and why the work mattered.

Include:

- Industry and operating context.
- Buyer or stakeholder type.
- Workflow, program, system, or transformation area.
- Why the problem was urgent.
- What made the environment complex.

Avoid:

- Generic industry setup.
- Long company background.
- Claims that cannot be evidenced.

### 2. Operational Complexity Pattern

Purpose: Identify the reusable pattern TKO recognized.

Include:

- Cross-team coordination problem.
- Workflow visibility failure.
- Decision latency.
- Governance breakdown.
- Program execution risk.
- AI adoption or control failure where relevant.

This section should make the case study reusable for buyers in other industries.

### 3. What Was Invisible

Purpose: Show what leadership, teams, or systems could not see.

Include:

- Hidden handoffs.
- Missing or disputed facts.
- Work stalled between systems or teams.
- Unused operational context.
- Delayed or unclear escalation points.
- Manual workarounds.
- Gaps between reporting and reality.

This is the core category proof. If nothing was invisible, the case study is not a TKO case study.

### 4. What Was Stalled

Purpose: Show the operational consequence of invisibility.

Include:

- Transformation progress.
- Workflow throughput.
- Decision-making.
- AI adoption.
- Program milestones.
- Customer, member, provider, advisor, or operator response.
- Compliance or governance movement.

Distinguish stalled activity from stalled outcomes. Busy teams are not proof of progress.

### 5. Operational Truth Framework Application

Purpose: Demonstrate TKO's method.

Use the framework explicitly:

1. What was supposed to happen?
2. What actually happened?
3. Where did the work stall?
4. What was known but unused?
5. What should the next action have been?
6. Where could AI help without taking control?
7. How was success measured?

Do not present the framework as a workshop. Present it as the diagnostic spine that produced operational truth.

### 6. System Built

Purpose: Explain the operating system, workflow model, governance mechanism, decision logic, or visibility layer created.

Include whichever elements apply:

- Workflow redesign.
- Decision-system design.
- Relationship or work memory layer.
- Source-aware facts.
- Operational state model.
- Visibility gap closure.
- Canonical next-action queue.
- Human approval model.
- Alerting or reliability model.
- Governance cadence.
- Measurement loop.
- Handoff documentation.

For advisory or employment-period work, this section may be titled "Operating Model Created" or "Work Performed" if no production system was built.

### 7. Outcome

Purpose: State what changed.

Include:

- Confirmed operational outcomes.
- Quantified outcomes only if evidence and attribution are clear.
- Qualitative outcomes when quantitative proof is not available.
- What became visible, governed, faster, or more reliable.

Avoid:

- Implied revenue impact without evidence.
- Sole-contribution claims for team-delivered work.
- Commercial outcomes for RachelDelray until measured.

### 8. Evidence Available

Purpose: Make proof status explicit.

Include:

- Source code.
- Database migrations.
- Public resume or LinkedIn claims.
- Internal artifacts if publishable.
- Founder confirmation.
- Client-approved metrics.
- Screenshots or workflow diagrams if approved.
- Case documentation.

This section can be public-facing in summarized form or internal-only depending on evidence sensitivity.

### 9. Evidence Missing / Gated

Purpose: Prevent overclaiming and identify proof gaps.

Include:

- Named client attribution not approved.
- Metrics not cleared for publication.
- Baseline data unavailable.
- Independent verification unavailable.
- Business outcomes not yet measured.
- Operator consent pending.

Treat this section as proof governance, not a disclaimer dump.

### 10. Related Problems

Purpose: Connect the case to TKO's problem architecture.

Select from:

- Transformation Recovery.
- Workflow Visibility Failure.
- Decision Latency.
- Program Execution Failure.
- Governance Breakdown.
- AI Adoption Failure.
- Cross-Team Coordination Failure.
- Operational Intelligence Design.

### 11. Related Service

Purpose: Tie proof to the engagement ladder.

Select one primary related service:

- Operational Intelligence Diagnostic.
- Operating System Build.
- Fractional Operating System Advisor.

The Diagnostic should appear as the default entry point even when the case proves Build or Fractional relevance.

### 12. CTA

Purpose: Route qualified buyers to the diagnostic entry point.

CTA: Start the Diagnostic.

Do not add competing CTAs inside case studies.

## Proof Levels

Use proof levels consistently across case study index cards, case study pages, and homepage proof modules.

| Level | Name | Definition | Where It Can Be Used |
|---|---|---|---|
| Level 1 | Method Proof | Demonstrates the Operational Truth Framework or system pattern, but does not prove client outcomes. | Insights, framework pages, early proof modules. |
| Level 2 | Production System Proof | A working system exists and operational capabilities can be evidenced. | RachelDelray system proof, Operating System Build page, homepage proof. |
| Level 3 | Anonymized Enterprise Proof | Enterprise work occurred, but client names or internal artifacts are gated. Metrics may be resume-level or anonymized. | Healthcare case studies, About page, proof summaries. |
| Level 4 | Attributed Outcome Proof | Client, context, metric, and role are approved for publication. | Full case studies, sales collateral, homepage proof. |
| Level 5 | Independently Verifiable Proof | Outcomes can be verified through third-party, client-published, or audited evidence. | Highest-value proof modules and enterprise sales materials. |

Proof level should be visible internally for governance. Public display may use simpler labels such as "production system," "anonymized enterprise work," or "client-approved outcome."

## Attribution Rules

### Founder-Built First-Party Product

Can include:

- Full technical and operational detail.
- System architecture.
- Source-code-supported capability claims.
- Screenshots or workflow descriptions where appropriate.

Requires permission:

- Operator name.
- Operator identity.
- Business outcomes tied to the operator.
- Testimonials.

Rules:

- Do not claim commercial impact unless measured.
- Operational proof is valid even when revenue proof is missing.

### Employment-Period Work

Can include:

- Anonymized organization description.
- Resume-level scope and metrics if founder confirms they are public and NDA-safe.
- Role description framed accurately.
- Pattern and operating problem.

Requires permission:

- Client or employer names beyond public resume use.
- Internal program details.
- Metrics beyond public resume or LinkedIn claims.
- Screenshots, artifacts, or internal documents.

Rules:

- Do not imply TKO delivered the work as a client engagement if it occurred during employment.
- Do not imply sole authorship for team-delivered outcomes.
- Use language such as "led," "contributed to," or "worked across" only when accurate.

### Client Work Performed By TKO

Can include when approved:

- Client name.
- Problem.
- Scope.
- Deliverables.
- Outcomes.
- Testimonial.
- Before/after metrics.

Requires permission:

- All client-identifying details unless contract permits publication.
- Metrics and quoted statements.
- Screenshots, artifacts, and operational diagrams.

Rules:

- Confirm publication rights before drafting public copy.
- Maintain a private proof file for evidence custody.

### Internal Prototype Or Proof Of Concept

Can include:

- Method demonstrated.
- System pattern.
- Technical learning.

Must not include:

- Client outcome claims.
- Market proof claims.
- Revenue or adoption claims unless measured.

## Anonymization Rules

Use anonymization when client names, employers, operators, internal artifacts, or metrics are not cleared.

### Naming

- Use industry plus operating context, not company name.
- Example structure: "Large healthcare organization," "Healthcare payer/provider network," "Wealth management platform team," "Capital markets operations environment."

### Role Description

- Describe the buyer or stakeholder type without exposing identity.
- Example: "VP Operations sponsor," "transformation leadership team," "clinical and operational stakeholders."

### Metrics

- Use ranges when exact figures are sensitive.
- Use resume-cited metrics only after founder confirmation.
- If metrics are directionally known but not cleared, state the operational change without a number.

### Artifacts

- Do not publish screenshots, diagrams, data exports, tickets, internal documents, system names, or vendor details unless cleared.
- Redact names, IDs, URLs, record counts, and proprietary workflow labels.

### Industry Specificity

- Healthcare can be specific by domain when safe: prior authorization, care management, interoperability, utilization management.
- Financial Services can be specific by function when safe: wealth platform operations, trade support, settlement, portfolio operations.
- Avoid proprietary product, client, or platform names unless approved.

### Attribution Notes

Every draft case study should include an internal attribution note:

- Work type.
- Publication status.
- Approved claims.
- Gated claims.
- Missing evidence.
- Required approvals.

## Case Study Index Requirements

Each case study card should include:

- Case title.
- Industry.
- Operating problem.
- Proof level.
- Related service.
- One-sentence structural pattern, not a final marketing claim.
- CTA or link path that ultimately routes to the Diagnostic.

Do not publish empty future case studies. Placeholders may exist only in internal planning documents.
