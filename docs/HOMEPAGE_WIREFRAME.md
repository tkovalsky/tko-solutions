# Homepage Wireframe

Status: Phase 2 build asset
Source of truth: `docs/TKO_ARCHITECTURE_DECISIONS.md`, `docs/WEBSITE_ARCHITECTURE.md`
Purpose: Define homepage structure, content logic, proof requirements, and conversion flow. This is not final copy.

---

## Page Job

The homepage must position TKO as an Operational Intelligence Systems firm and route qualified buyers toward the Operational Intelligence Diagnostic.

Primary audience: healthcare operations and transformation leaders.

Secondary audience: regulated enterprise operations leaders in Financial Services, Wealth and Asset Management, and Capital Markets Operations.

Primary conversion action: Start the Diagnostic.

The homepage should make the buyer feel recognized around one problem: their organization cannot see where work is actually failing.

---

## Section 1: Hero

### Section Purpose

Immediately establish the category, the core operating problem, and the diagnostic entry point.

### Headline Objective

Communicate that TKO helps complex organizations see where work is actually failing and convert that visibility into a system of action. The headline should lead with Operational Intelligence and operational failure visibility, not AI, healthcare consulting, PMO, or generic transformation.

### Supporting Narrative

The narrative should describe the gap between visible activity and invisible failure:

- Teams are working.
- Systems are producing data.
- Governance meetings are happening.
- Transformation or AI investments are active.
- Leadership still cannot see where work stalls or what should happen next.

Healthcare should be the first example context. Financial Services may be referenced as a secondary regulated operations context, but not at equal weight.

### Proof Required

- Short credibility signal that TKO has worked across healthcare transformation, prior authorization, interoperability, enterprise program recovery, and operational systems.
- Reference to production system-building capability without overclaiming commercial outcomes.
- No detailed metrics in the hero unless attribution is confirmed.

### CTA Placement

Primary CTA in the hero: Start the Diagnostic.

No secondary CTA. Supporting proof links may appear as navigation or lower-page links, not as competing hero CTAs.

---

## Section 2: Problem

### Section Purpose

Make the buyer's invisible operating failure concrete before introducing the method.

### Headline Objective

Frame the problem as a visibility failure inside complex work, not a lack of effort, tools, or meetings.

### Supporting Narrative

Show the buyer symptoms TKO solves:

- Work crosses teams and systems.
- Decisions depend on facts that are scattered or disputed.
- Dependencies become visible too late.
- Governance meetings do not improve execution.
- AI pilots do not reach trusted operational use.
- Reporting shows activity but not where work fails.

Use healthcare examples first:

- Prior authorization queues.
- Care management handoffs.
- Payer/provider workflow complexity.
- Administrative burden.
- Interoperability-driven operating change.

Use Financial Services examples as supporting parallels:

- Trade support and settlement handoffs.
- Wealth management platform operations.
- Portfolio operations and advisor workflow visibility.

### Proof Required

- No case study details required in this section.
- Use operational pattern evidence rather than claims of broad market coverage.

### CTA Placement

No full CTA required unless section length demands a repeated in-page action. If used, repeat the same primary CTA only.

---

## Section 3: Operational Truth Framework

### Section Purpose

Introduce TKO's method for turning invisible failure into operational truth.

### Headline Objective

Position the framework as the disciplined way to compare what is supposed to happen with what actually happens.

### Supporting Narrative

Structure the section around the framework questions:

1. What is supposed to happen?
2. What actually happens?
3. Where does the work stall?
4. What is known but unused?
5. What should the next action be?
6. Where can AI help without taking control?
7. How is success measured?

The section should show that TKO does not begin with tooling. It begins by identifying failure points, decision gaps, and visibility gaps in real work.

### Proof Required

- Reference to framework use across healthcare modernization and production operating system design.
- Avoid implying the framework is a generic workshop. It must feel diagnostic, evidence-based, and operational.

### CTA Placement

Place a compact CTA after the framework explanation if the page has enough depth by this point. CTA text remains Start the Diagnostic.

---

## Section 4: Operational Intelligence System

### Section Purpose

Explain what TKO builds or designs after operational truth is established.

### Headline Objective

Show that Operational Intelligence Systems convert scattered signals into governed facts, visible state, canonical next actions, human approval, and measured outcomes.

### Supporting Narrative

Present the system pattern as a flow:

1. Signals from workflows, teams, systems, and operators.
2. Governed memory of what is known.
3. Source-aware facts and operational state.
4. Visibility into gaps, stalls, dependencies, and risk.
5. One trusted next action or escalation.
6. Human approval before action.
7. Measurement loop that shows whether execution improves.

AI should appear only as a controlled mechanism inside the system, not as the product category.

### Proof Required

- RachelDelray operational system as proof of the system pattern.
- Healthcare modernization experience as proof of enterprise complexity.
- Clear distinction between operational proof and commercially measured outcomes when applicable.

### CTA Placement

Inline CTA after the system model, especially for visitors who already understand the need.

---

## Section 5: Proof

### Section Purpose

Establish credibility without overclaiming attribution or unverified outcomes.

### Headline Objective

Demonstrate that TKO recognizes and solves the same operational failure pattern across complex domains.

### Supporting Narrative

Organize proof by pattern and evidence level:

- Healthcare prior authorization workflow modernization.
- Enterprise care management modernization.
- Healthcare interoperability platform work.
- RachelDelray Operational Intelligence System as production system proof.
- Financial Services, Wealth Management, and Capital Markets experience as secondary pattern recognition.

The proof should reinforce the category thesis: different industries, same hidden operating failures.

### Proof Required

- Case study cards or proof summaries tagged by industry, problem, method, and evidence status.
- Attribution notes where metrics are employment-attributed, anonymized, or gated.
- No named clients without permission.
- No commercial claims for RachelDelray beyond confirmed operational capability.

### CTA Placement

CTA after proof block: Start the Diagnostic.

Optional internal links to case studies may appear, but they should not compete with the primary CTA.

---

## Section 6: Service Ladder

### Section Purpose

Clarify how buyers engage TKO without creating offer confusion.

### Headline Objective

Present one service ladder with the Diagnostic as the entry point and Build/Fractional as downstream paths.

### Supporting Narrative

Show the three rungs:

1. Operational Intelligence Diagnostic: paid entry point for finding where work is failing.
2. Operating System Build: downstream implementation path for designing and building the system.
3. Fractional Operating System Advisor: downstream advisory path for sustaining governance and execution.

The section must avoid presenting capability modules as separate offers.

### Proof Required

- Pricing posture for Diagnostic should be visible enough to qualify serious buyers on the service page path, but the homepage may summarize rather than fully price.
- Build and Fractional should be framed as conversion offers, not cold-entry offers.

### CTA Placement

Primary CTA after the ladder: Start the Diagnostic.

Links to service pages may exist if pages are built, but the action remains diagnostic intake.

---

## Section 7: Founder Credibility

### Section Purpose

Show founder credibility as cross-industry operational pattern recognition, not autobiography.

### Headline Objective

Position Todd's credibility around recognizing the same operational failures across healthcare, financial services, wealth management, capital markets, and enterprise operations.

### Supporting Narrative

Structure around operating environments:

- Healthcare: prior authorization, care management, interoperability, transformation, AI governance.
- Financial Services: hedge fund operations, institutional asset management, wealth management, advisor technology.
- Capital Markets and Enterprise Operations: trade support, settlement operations, program recovery, operating model design.

The page should show that the category is Operational Intelligence and healthcare is the launch wedge because proof is strongest there.

### Proof Required

- Resume-level credibility summarized without overclaiming client-specific attribution.
- Reference to enterprise transformation and program leadership.
- Link to About page if available.

### CTA Placement

CTA at the end of the credibility section only if the final CTA is not immediately adjacent. Same CTA text.

---

## Section 8: Final CTA

### Section Purpose

Convert qualified readers who recognize the problem and trust the method.

### Headline Objective

Frame the Diagnostic as the next step for leaders who need operational truth before deciding what to fix.

### Supporting Narrative

Summarize what the Diagnostic gives the buyer:

- Evidence of where work is failing.
- Visibility into stalls, gaps, and handoffs.
- Ranked recovery opportunities.
- A 90-day recovery roadmap.
- A basis for deciding whether to build, govern, or stop work.

### Proof Required

- Reinforce fixed-scope, executive-ready, paid diagnostic positioning.
- Avoid open-ended consultation language.

### CTA Placement

Final primary CTA: Start the Diagnostic.

Destination: diagnostic intake.
