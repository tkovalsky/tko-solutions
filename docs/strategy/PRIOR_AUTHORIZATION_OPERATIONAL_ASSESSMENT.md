# Prior Authorization Operational Assessment

Status: Tier-1 TKO opportunity
Purpose: Preserve the prior authorization assessment wedge as a healthcare workflow modernization opportunity, not a product/platform thesis.

## Tier-1 Classification

Prior Authorization Operational Assessment is a Tier-1 TKO opportunity because it sits directly at the intersection of:

- Human APIs
- Administrative Burden
- Healthcare Transformation
- AI Adoption
- Operational Assessments

Those are the same intellectual property pillars TKO is already building around.

## Strategic Position

TKO should not initially pursue:

- Gold Card platforms
- Prior authorization automation products
- Payer technology products
- FHIR infrastructure products
- Claims adjudication platforms

These are large-market categories with significant implementation complexity, long sales cycles, and product/platform expectations.

TKO's wedge is operational quality improvement.

Core insight:

> Gold Card eligibility is not the problem. Operational quality is the problem.

Organizations become Gold Card eligible because:

- Requests are complete.
- Workflows are consistent.
- Documentation quality is high.
- Denial rates are low.
- Operational variation is controlled.

The business opportunity exists before Gold Card eligibility.

## Core Market Observation

Practice administrators do not wake up asking for:

- Gold Card programs.
- FHIR integrations.
- Prior authorization platforms.

They ask:

- Why are we getting denied?
- Why does authorization take so long?
- Why is staff spending hours on manual work?
- Why do some providers get approved and others do not?
- Why do certain payers constantly create friction?
- Why is work dependent on specific people?

This distinction is a core GTM insight. The buyer feels administrative burden, denial friction, staff dependency, and workflow inconsistency before they care about platform categories.

## Offer: Prior Authorization Operational Assessment

Position this as a healthcare workflow modernization assessment focused on:

- Administrative burden.
- Denial reduction.
- Workflow quality.
- Operational consistency.
- Human dependency risk.

Do not position it as:

- Automation software.
- AI platform.
- Gold Card software.

### Inputs

- PA submissions.
- Denial reasons.
- Specialty.
- Payer mix.
- Workflow documentation.
- Staffing model.

### Outputs

#### Administrative Burden Analysis

Measures:

- Volume.
- Effort.
- Turnaround time.
- Exception frequency.

#### Denial Analysis

Measures:

- Documentation issues.
- Coding issues.
- Eligibility issues.
- Medical necessity issues.

#### Exception Analysis

Measures:

- Repeat patterns.
- Recurring bottlenecks.
- Recurring escalation paths.

#### Human API Risk Analysis

Questions:

- Who knows payer-specific rules?
- Who handles exceptions?
- Who performs escalations?
- Who trains staff?
- What happens if they leave?

#### Gold Card Readiness Assessment

Measures:

- Estimated approval quality.
- Workflow standardization.
- Documentation quality.
- Operational maturity.

Gold Card readiness is an output of operational quality. It is not the product.

## Human APIs In Prior Authorization

Every authorization team typically contains one or two people who:

- Know payer-specific workflows.
- Know escalation paths.
- Know exception handling.
- Know documentation requirements.

Organizations often believe they have a workflow.

In reality they have a person.

This is a recurring Human API pattern and one of TKO's strongest healthcare proof points.

## Prior Authorization Assessment Prototype

This is a future implementation opportunity only. It is not current application work.

- Provider
- Payer
- Authorization
- Denial Reason
- Exception
- Staff Member

Capabilities:

- Track denials.
- Categorize denials.
- Identify repeat patterns.

- No FHIR.
