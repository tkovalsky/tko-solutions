# Ideal Customer Profile: Human API Risk Assessment

> **Status — supporting reference, reframed 2026-06-25.** "Human API" is a **finding type and
> diagnostic lens**, not a standalone offer, separate assessment, or pricing tier. The offers are
> the **Operational Recovery Assessment** and the **Prior Authorization Operational Assessment**
> (see the authoritative [`/CURRENT_REALITY.md`](../CURRENT_REALITY.md)). Read the buyer profiles
> below as the ICP for those assessments — Human API dependency is one pain the assessment surfaces.

## Primary ICP

Healthcare payer operations organizations where high-value work is slowed by manual coordination, unclear decision rights, exception handling, and dependency on experienced individuals.

## Best-Fit Environments

- Prior authorization operations
- Utilization management
- Care management
- Provider operations
- Member operations
- Claims-adjacent operating teams
- Transformation offices
- Administrative burden reduction programs
- AI or automation programs struggling with adoption

## Buyer Profiles

### Economic Buyer

- COO
- CIO
- Chief Transformation Officer
- VP Operations
- VP Utilization Management
- VP Care Management
- VP Provider Operations

What they care about:

- Operational risk
- Throughput and delay
- Transformation credibility
- Administrative burden
- AI readiness
- Regulatory and audit exposure
- Sponsor confidence
- Enterprise value and exit readiness when founder or key-employee dependency affects transferability.

### Champion

- Director of Operations
- Transformation Office lead
- Program or portfolio leader
- Operational excellence leader
- Health tech delivery leader
- Consulting engagement partner

What they care about:

- Making hidden blockers visible
- Getting executive alignment
- Turning anecdotes into evidence
- Creating a practical recovery plan
- Reducing dependence on one or two overloaded people

### Interview Participants

- Directors
- Managers
- Front-line operators
- Clinical reviewers
- Program managers
- Analysts
- Reporting owners
- System administrators
- Informal escalation owners

## Buying Triggers

- A critical employee leaves or becomes overloaded.
- A workflow slows down when a specific person is unavailable.
- An AI initiative cannot move beyond pilots because workflow reality is unclear.
- A transformation program is reporting progress but leaders do not trust the status.
- Administrative burden persists despite automation or vendor investment.
- Prior authorization denials, delays, payer friction, and staff dependency are creating operational quality pressure.
- Prior authorization, UM, or care management operations depend on undocumented exception handling.
- Work relies on spreadsheets, email chains, meetings, and personal routing.
- A new executive inherits a messy operating environment and needs the truth fast.
- A founder, owner, healthcare practice group, PE sponsor, search fund, broker, or M&A advisor needs to understand whether tribal knowledge, undocumented workflows, or missing organizational memory will reduce business value.

## Disqualifiers

- Buyer wants generic knowledge management software.
- Buyer wants a low-cost employee survey.
- Organization is not willing to allow interviews with operators and managers.
- Sponsor only wants technology recommendations without current-state discovery.
- Sponsor wants to blame individuals rather than reduce system dependency.
- No executive owner exists for remediation.

## Secondary ICP

### Health Tech

Best fit:

- Vendors selling into payer/provider operations.
- Companies whose implementations fail because client workflow and decision rights are unclear.
- Product teams needing operational context before AI, automation, or workflow design.

Use case:

- Human API assessment as pre-implementation discovery or client adoption risk assessment.

### Consulting Firms

Best fit:

- Healthcare consulting firms delivering transformation, operating model, AI, or administrative burden programs.
- Firms that need a specialist diagnostic layer to expose operational truth before recommendations.

Use case:

- TKO as a specialist partner for workflow dependency, decision-layer, and operating intelligence assessments.

### Enterprise Value & Exit Readiness Adjacency

Best fit:

- Business owners and founders preparing for sale, succession, or scale.
- Healthcare practice owners and multi-location healthcare groups.
- Private equity operating partners and portfolio company COOs.
- Search funds, business brokers, M&A advisors, and fractional operators.

Use case:

- Future-state assessment of Human API risk, organizational dependency, operational memory, workflow scalability, AI readiness, and enterprise value risk. This is not the primary wedge today.

## Message-Market Fit

Strongest message:

"Your workflows do not fail because people lack knowledge. They fail because the organization depends on specific people as the operating system."

Healthcare-specific message:

"Administrative burden is often hidden decision latency. The question is not only how to automate work, but where decisions, exceptions, and escalation paths currently live inside people."

AI-specific message:

"AI cannot improve a workflow that the organization cannot describe, govern, or route without a Human API."

Prior authorization-specific message:

"Gold Card eligibility is not the problem. Operational quality is the problem. The assessment shows why requests are denied, why authorization takes too long, where staff effort is consumed, which payer patterns repeat, and where the workflow depends on specific people."
