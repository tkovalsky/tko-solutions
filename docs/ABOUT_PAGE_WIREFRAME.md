---

## Page Job

The About page must explain why TKO can recognize operational failure patterns across complex enterprises and design systems that make those failures visible and actionable.

The page should not be a founder biography. It should use founder credibility to support the category thesis: organizations cannot see where work is actually failing, and that failure pattern repeats across industries.

---

## Section 1: Category Thesis

### Purpose

Anchor the page in TKO's point of view before discussing founder background.

### Content Architecture

The section should explain:

- Complex organizations rarely fail because nobody is working.
- They fail because work crosses teams, systems, decisions, and governance structures faster than leaders can observe it.
- Operational Intelligence Systems exist to make that work visible, governed, actionable, and measurable.
- Healthcare is the launch wedge because current proof and operating complexity are strongest there.
- Financial Services, Wealth Management, Capital Markets, and enterprise operations show the same pattern in different forms.

### Proof Required

- No personal narrative required yet.
- Use industry pattern framing only.

### CTA Placement

No CTA in this opening section unless page design requires a persistent header CTA.

## Section 2: Pattern Recognition Across Industries

### Purpose

Show the repeated operating failure pattern across healthcare, financial services, wealth management, capital markets, and enterprise operations.

### Content Architecture

Use a comparative structure:

| Industry Context | Visible Symptoms | Underlying Operational Failure |
|---|---|---|
| Healthcare | Administrative burden, delayed reviews, care management handoffs, AI adoption friction, compliance-driven workflow pressure. | Work stalls across clinical, operational, technical, and governance boundaries without a trusted visibility layer. |
| Financial Services | Platform modernization risk, operational handoffs, loan administration complexity, workflow exceptions. | Teams rely on fragmented systems and manual coordination to move regulated work. |
| Wealth Management | Advisor workflows, client service handoffs, platform adoption gaps, relationship context fragmentation. | Relationship-intensive work depends on context that is not captured as operational intelligence. |
| Capital Markets | Trade support, settlement, exception handling, portfolio operations, institutional workflow complexity. | Decisions and exceptions move through high-risk operational flows where late visibility creates enterprise risk. |
| Enterprise Operations | Program execution drift, governance meetings, dependency risk, transformation fatigue. | Operating models exist on paper, but decision rights, visibility, and measurement are not embedded into daily work. |

### Proof Required

- Founder experience should be used to validate pattern recognition.
- Avoid claiming equal proof depth across all industries.
- Healthcare proof remains dominant.

### CTA Placement

No CTA required.

## Section 3: Founder Credibility Architecture

### Purpose

Translate Todd's background into buyer-relevant credibility.

### Content Architecture

Organize credibility by operating problem, not chronology:

1. Healthcare transformation and modernization.
2. Prior authorization and administrative burden.
3. Care management and enterprise healthcare operations.
4. Interoperability and compliance-driven operating change.
5. Financial Services, Wealth Management, and Capital Markets operations.
6. Product ownership, program leadership, PMO transformation, and operating model design.
7. Operational Intelligence System design and production system-building.

### Detail Strategy

For each credibility area, include:

- Operating environment.
- Complexity pattern.
- Type of work performed.
- What it proves about TKO's ability to diagnose operational failure.

Do not structure the section as a resume timeline.

### Proof Required

- Use resume-level claims only where publishable.
- Employment-period work should be anonymized unless attribution is confirmed.
- Avoid sole-contribution claims for team-delivered outcomes.

### CTA Placement

CTA can appear after this section if the page is long: Start the Diagnostic.

## Section 4: Healthcare Pattern

### Purpose

Make healthcare the strongest proof-backed launch context.

### Content Architecture

Cover the recurring healthcare operating pattern:

- Prior authorization and utilization management workflows depend on tiered decisions, clinical complexity, compliance, and human review.
- Care management and provider operations depend on cross-functional handoffs and timely visibility.
- Interoperability programs require operational adoption, not only technical compliance.
- Healthcare AI adoption fails when governance, workflow, and human approval are not designed together.

### Proof Required

- Reference prior authorization modernization.
- Reference enterprise care management modernization.
- Reference interoperability platform work.
- Include attribution and anonymization caveats where needed.

### CTA Placement

Optional CTA to Diagnostic if the page includes a healthcare-specific proof block nearby.

## Section 5: Financial Services, Wealth Management, And Capital Markets Pattern

### Purpose

Show secondary market credibility without diluting the healthcare wedge.

### Content Architecture

Frame these contexts as supporting evidence of the category:

- Regulated work depends on operational truth, not just reporting.
- Relationship-intensive workflows require durable context and next-action logic.
- Platform modernization creates dependency and governance risk.
- Capital markets operations require exception visibility, decision clarity, and operational control.

### Proof Required

- Founder career history can support credibility.
- Do not create separate offers or imply equal launch priority.
- Do not overstate current proof if no current case study exists.

### CTA Placement

No separate CTA. Keep the page-wide CTA focused on the Diagnostic.

## Section 6: Operational Intelligence Method

### Purpose

Connect credibility to TKO's repeatable method.

### Content Architecture

Introduce the Operational Truth Framework as the bridge between pattern recognition and system design:

- What is supposed to happen?
- What actually happens?
- Where does work stall?
- What is known but unused?
- What should happen next?
- Where can AI help without taking control?
- How is success measured?

Then show how the framework leads to Operational Intelligence Systems:

- Signals.
- Governed facts.
- Operational state.
- Visibility gaps.
- Canonical next action.
- Human approval.
- Measurement loop.

### Proof Required

- RachelDelray system as production proof of the system pattern.
- Healthcare modernization background as enterprise complexity proof.

### CTA Placement

CTA after method section: Start the Diagnostic.

## Section 7: Proof Architecture

### Purpose

Explain how TKO handles proof responsibly.

### Content Architecture

Proof should be grouped into tiers:

1. Production system proof: first-party system with inspectable operational capability.
2. Anonymized enterprise work: healthcare modernization, prior authorization, care management, interoperability.
3. Career credibility: financial services, wealth management, capital markets, enterprise operations.
4. Gated or future proof: named case studies, quantified outcomes, client-approved attribution.

### Proof Rules

- Name only what can be named.
- Attribute employment-period work carefully.
- Do not claim unverified commercial outcomes.
- Use evidence status as a credibility signal, not a weakness.

### CTA Placement

CTA after proof: Start the Diagnostic.

### Purpose

Move from founder credibility to buyer action.

### Content Architecture

- If the buyer has a stalled transformation, invisible workflow, failed AI adoption, or governance problem, the next step is not a broad consulting conversation.
- The next step is a fixed-scope Diagnostic that establishes operational truth and ranks recovery opportunities.
