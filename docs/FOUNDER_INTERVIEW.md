## Positioning (4 questions)

**1. What do you say when someone asks "what does TKO do?" — in one sentence, out loud, with no prep?**

The documents have multiple answers. The one that comes out naturally in conversation is the one to build from. Write or record what you actually say.

**2. Are you selling as Todd Kovalsky (individual advisor) or as TKO Solutions (firm)?**

This affects proposals, invoicing, LinkedIn, and how introductions are made. Most solo consultants at the $25k+ level sell as themselves with a firm name for invoicing. Do you want TKO to feel like a firm with future hiring capacity, or a named advisor practice?

**3. Is healthcare the required primary market, or is it the most credible first path?**

If a financial services operations leader calls tomorrow with an active problem, do you take the meeting? Knowing whether you are committed to healthcare-only or healthcare-first changes the offer copy and outreach strategy.

**4. What is the one sentence you want a former colleague to use when they refer you?**

This is the referral-ready pitch. It should be specific enough that the person hearing it knows immediately whether they are the right buyer.

## Target Customer (3 questions)

**5. Who is the most specific buyer you can picture paying TKO $25k in the next 90 days?**

Not a job title — a real type of person at a real type of organization. What has just happened in their world that makes them ready to act? What does their morning look like the day they decide to call?

**6. Have you had any informal conversation since June 2026 where someone said "I wish I could hire you for this"?**

That conversation is the warmest lead. If yes, describe the problem they were dealing with.

**7. Who in your network is most likely to introduce you to a buyer — a former colleague, a consulting partner, a vendor contact, or a health-tech founder?**

Understanding the referral path determines where to spend the first 10 hours of outreach effort.

## Buyer Urgency (3 questions)

**8. Do you have any active outreach, referrals, or consulting conversations already in flight?**

Even informal ones count. "A former colleague mentioned a health plan struggling with their AI program" is a real lead. Knowing whether you are starting from zero changes the 30-day plan.

**9. What buyer trigger events do you hear most often when talking to former healthcare contacts?**

Not what the documents say — what you actually hear when you talk to people in your network right now. The most common complaints are the right hooks for outreach messages.

**10. Is there a specific regulatory event, deadline, or market pressure (e.g., CMS requirements, AI governance mandates) that is creating urgency among your target buyers right now?**

Urgency that is external and time-bound is far easier to sell into than general pain.

## Pricing (3 questions)

**11. What is your actual pricing floor — the minimum you would accept to sign a first engagement?**

There is a contradiction in the documents: `TKO_ROADMAP.md` says $5k–$15k; `TKO_CLIENT_ACQUISITION_OPERATING_PLAN.md` says $25k. These require different buyers and different authority levels. Pick one. Write it down. It is now the floor.

**12. Would you accept equity, deferred payment, or a case study trade for a below-floor engagement?**

Some early-stage consultants accept reduced fees in exchange for the right to publish a case study. Is that on the table, and if so, what conditions would make it worthwhile?

**13. What monthly revenue number changes your decision-making — where TKO stops being a side project and becomes the primary focus?**

The roadmap says "$10k+/month by August–September 2026." Is that still true? What does the number need to be to justify reducing commitment to your current employer?

**15. For a 3-week diagnostic delivered remotely, what does the client experience look like — synchronous calls, asynchronous document review, or a combination?**

This determines whether timezone, travel, or calendar constraints affect who you can serve.

**16. Is TKO currently a solo operation, or do you have any partners, subcontractors, or support capacity?**

Knowing this determines whether engagements larger than a diagnostic are realistic in the near term.

## Proof (4 questions)

**17. Is Rachel actively using the RachelDelray.com system daily, and has it produced any measurable business outcomes?**

The system is operationally proven (it works as designed). Whether it has produced commercial outcomes — leads converted, appointments created, transactions influenced — determines how it can be presented in a case study. What outcomes exist today?

**18. Are the prior authorization metrics (40–60% manual review reduction, $200M+ annualized value, 15–25% productivity improvement) subject to NDA or attribution restrictions?**

These are the most powerful proof assets in the portfolio. Before using them in any proposal or published case study, you need to know exactly what you can say and how specifically you can say it.

**19. Is there any client, employer, or project where you could obtain explicit permission to publish a named case study?**

Even "a mid-market health plan" with implied permission from a former colleague would accelerate credibility faster than anonymized examples. Is there one?

**20. Are there any deliverables from past engagements — framework documents, assessment templates, readout decks — that you own and could adapt as TKO intellectual property?**

Former employers may own deliverables created during employment. Understand what you can reuse.

**22. Is there any in-progress work at your current employer that constitutes TKO-equivalent work you are not billing for?**

**23. What is the current state of the domain and legal structure for TKO Solutions — is the domain live, is an LLC formed, and is a business bank account open?**

These are not blockers for first outreach, but they are blockers for signing a $25k contract.
