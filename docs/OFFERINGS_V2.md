## Offer 1 — Fastest to Sell

### Name
**Operational Intelligence Diagnostic**

Healthcare framing: **Healthcare Transformation Recovery Diagnostic**
Administrative burden framing: **Administrative Burden Reduction Diagnostic**

Use the framing that matches the buyer's stated problem. The underlying method is identical.

### Customer
Healthcare operations leader who cannot explain where work is actually stalling.

**Most likely title:** VP Operations · VP Utilization Management · VP Provider Operations · Chief Transformation Officer · CIO · Health-tech COO

**Org type:** Health plan, payer operations group, provider operations, health-tech vendor, or healthcare consulting firm with an active client problem.

**Not the right buyer:** Anyone who needs to convince their organization that a problem exists. The right buyer already knows there is a problem and needs a trusted external perspective to surface what it actually is.

### Trigger Event
One or more of these has recently become visible or urgent:
- A sponsor, board member, or client is asking why transformation isn't producing results
- An AI initiative has been running for 12+ months without measurable adoption
- Administrative burden is growing despite technology investment
- A new leader inherited a stalled program and needs to establish what is actually true
- A regulatory deadline (CMS, state, accreditation) is forcing accountability
- Operational metrics are being debated instead of trusted

### Problem
Leadership is investing in transformation, AI, or operational improvement but cannot see where work is actually stalling. The constraint is not the technology. It is the inability to distinguish what is supposed to happen from what actually happens — and to do anything useful with that knowledge once it is visible.

### Deliverables
1. **Operational Truth Assessment** — "What is supposed to happen vs. what actually happens" map for the target workflow or program area, using the Operational Truth Framework.
2. **Failure-Point and Manual-Handoff Inventory** — Where work stalls, where humans are doing work that should be automated or visible, where handoffs create invisible gaps.
3. **Visibility Gap Map** — Where leadership cannot see what is happening and why, using which systems and signals.
4. **Administrative Burden and Leakage Inventory** — Where the organization pays more (time, cost, delay, rework) than the workflow requires.
5. **AI Opportunity and Control Model** — Where AI can reduce burden without governance risk, and where human decision authority must remain.
6. **Top 10 Recovery Opportunities** — Ranked by operational value, implementation risk, and effort.
7. **90-Day Recovery Roadmap** — Sequenced actions with owners, gates, and success criteria.
8. **Executive Readout** — 60-minute live presentation of findings and recommendations.

### Timeline
3 weeks from engagement start.
- Week 1: Stakeholder interviews and artifact review.
- Week 2: Analysis, failure mapping, opportunity ranking.
- Week 3: Readout preparation and delivery.

Maximum 6–8 interviews. Scope creep begins when interview count rises above 10 or workflow areas exceed 2.

### Pricing Range
**$25,000 fixed fee** (recommended floor)

Range: $20,000–$35,000 based on workflow complexity and stakeholder access required.

Payment terms: 50% at engagement start, 50% at executive readout delivery.

Range note: Do not price below $20k. Below that, the engagement cannot be delivered at quality and the buyer will not take the findings seriously. The $5k–$15k figure in TKO_ROADMAP.md is inconsistent with the target buyer and should be retired.

### Outcomes
- Leadership has an evidence-based view of where work is actually stalling — something they could not produce internally.
- A prioritized 90-day recovery plan replaces a general transformation strategy.
- The engagement either closes with internal alignment on what to fix, or produces a natural follow-on proposal for implementation.

### Sales Narrative
"Your teams can't tell you where the work is actually stalling. They can tell you what systems they use, what the backlog looks like, and what meetings they attend — but not why the transformation isn't producing results. I run a 3-week diagnostic using a framework that asks seven questions your internal assessments miss. At the end you have the ten most valuable fixes ranked by effort and impact, a 90-day roadmap, and a brief you can present to your sponsor the same week. It's a fixed fee. No ambiguity about scope or cost."

### Included
- Up to 8 stakeholder interviews
- Review of existing workflow documentation, process maps, reporting, and operational data
- Application of the Operational Truth Framework
- All deliverables listed above
- One executive readout session
- 30 days of follow-up Q&A on findings

### Excluded
- Implementation of any recommendations
- Ongoing monitoring or advisory after follow-up window
- Technical architecture or software development
- Engagements spanning more than 2 workflow areas (requires scope adjustment)

### Proof Available Today
- The Operational Truth Framework (documented methodology, explainable in any sales call)
- RachelDelray.com forensic audit and stabilization as proof of the diagnostic pattern applied in production
- Healthcare program experience: $12M–$20M+ portfolios, multi-application complexity, PA/UM/care management domains (resume-attributed; confirm publishability before citing)
- Operational recovery case study pattern: silent failure discovery, root cause analysis, recovery sequencing

## Offer 2 — Highest Value

### Name
**Operating System Build**

Healthcare framing: **Healthcare Operating System Design and Implementation**

### Customer
An organization that has completed a diagnostic (internal or with TKO) and needs the identified workflow or decision problem actually fixed — not described.

**Most likely title:** COO · CIO · Chief Transformation Officer · Health-tech founder or VP Engineering · VP Operations with budget authority

**Org type:** Health plan with a funded transformation initiative · Health-tech company with an AI or workflow product that isn't operationally working · Mid-market organization that needs a senior external operator to design and build the system, not just consult on it.

**Sales note:** This offer should not be sold cold. It follows a diagnostic or a warm referral from someone who has seen TKO's work. The diagnostic creates the trust required for a $50k–$100k commitment.

### Trigger Event
- A diagnostic (TKO or internal) has identified the constraint, and the organization needs someone to build the fix — not write another strategy document
- An AI implementation has produced a product but no workflow adoption
- A workflow redesign has been designed but not operationalized
- A consulting firm engagement has produced a deck but no working system
- A new operating model has been defined but the decision system, queue, and measurement layer haven't been built

### Problem
The organization knows what needs to change. It does not have the internal capacity to convert that knowledge into a working decision system — one that captures signals, extracts facts, surfaces the right next action, keeps humans in control, and measures what happens.

### Deliverables
The specific deliverables depend on what the diagnostic or prior assessment identified. The following is the baseline engagement scope:

1. **Workflow redesign** — Target operating model for 1–2 workflow areas, incorporating decision rights, AI role, human approval requirements, and exception handling.
2. **Decision system design** — What triggers an action, what facts are required, what the canonical next action is, and who approves it. Deterministic, not AI-decided.
3. **Relationship/work memory layer** — How signals and updates are captured, structured, and made available to the decision layer. (Pattern: relationship updates → facts → source authority → supersession rules.)
4. **Intelligence gap acquisition** — What the organization doesn't know that matters, and how to systematically acquire it.
5. **Operator workflow** — What the human operator sees, does, and approves. Designed around human attention, not data completeness.
6. **Operational alerting and reliability** — What the system monitors, what failures it detects, how it surfaces problems before they become invisible.
7. **Measurement model** — How success is tracked: leading indicators, lagging outcomes, and the feedback loop between them.
8. **Handoff documentation** — What the internal team needs to operate and evolve the system after TKO's engagement closes.

What is not included unless scoped separately: software development, systems integration, database engineering, vendor selection, or management of internal implementation teams.

### Timeline
8–12 weeks.
- Weeks 1–2: Current-state validation and target operating model design.
- Weeks 3–6: Decision system design, operator workflow design, measurement model.
- Weeks 7–10: Build oversight, testing, and operator adoption.
- Weeks 11–12: Handoff, documentation, and close.

Note on "build": TKO designs the system and oversees its construction. Depending on the client's engineering capacity, TKO may provide implementation guidance, code review, or direct development. Scope this explicitly in the proposal.

### Pricing Range
**$50,000–$100,000 fixed fee**

Lower end: Single workflow area, well-defined scope, strong internal engineering capacity.
Upper end: Multiple workflow areas, AI integration, operator workflow design from scratch, TKO-led implementation.

Payment: 33% at start, 33% at midpoint milestone, 33% at close.

### Outcomes
- A working decision system that captures signals, surfaces canonical next actions, and keeps humans in control of final action.
- Operational alerting that makes silent failure visible.
- A measurement model that tracks whether the system is producing outcomes.
- Internal team equipped to operate the system without ongoing external dependency.

### Sales Narrative
"Strategy firms tell you what to build. Software shops build what you ask for. Neither leaves you with a working operating system — one that knows what the next action is, shows you why, puts the right person in the approval seat, and tells you when something is broken before you discover it yourself. That's what we build. It's a different kind of engagement: we start from what the diagnostic found, design the decision logic, and build the system that makes the recommendation actionable. You end up with something that runs, not something that sits in a slide deck."

### Included
- Workflow redesign and decision system design for up to 2 workflow areas
- Operator workflow design
- Intelligence gap acquisition model
- Operational alerting and reliability design
- Measurement model
- Build oversight (assumes client engineering capacity for implementation)
- Handoff documentation and operator training

### Excluded
- Direct software development beyond design and oversight (available as add-on scope)
- Management of vendor contracts or third-party integrations
- Ongoing advisory after engagement close (available as fractional retainer)
- Engagements without a defined prior diagnostic or equivalent current-state assessment

### Proof Available Today
- RachelDelray.com operating system: full build, code-backed, documented pattern from signals to outcome
- Architecture pattern: Signals → Relationship Memory → Facts → State → Canonical Action → Human Approval → Outcome (proven in production)
- Healthcare transformation experience at program scale
- Specific capabilities: relationship memory, fact extraction, source authority, canonical queue, intelligence gap detection, approval-gated AI drafts, operational alerting (all live in code)

### Proof Still Needed
- A second delivered operating system for a different client in a different domain — this is the proof that the pattern is reusable, not just that it worked once
- A published case study with measurable outcomes (appointments, throughput, cost reduction, adoption rate)

## Offer 3 — Recurring Advisory

### Name
**Fractional Operating System Advisor**

### Customer
An organization that has completed a diagnostic or build engagement and needs senior operational transformation leadership to maintain momentum — without a full-time executive hire.

Secondary path: A healthcare consulting firm that needs specialist delivery capacity on a client engagement.

**Most likely title:** COO · CIO · consulting partner (for their client) · VP Operations post-implementation

**Sales note:** This offer is difficult to sell cold to an organization with no prior TKO relationship. The primary path is conversion from a diagnostic or build engagement. The secondary path is a consulting firm referral.

### Trigger Event
- A TKO diagnostic or build engagement has closed and the buyer wants to retain momentum
- The operating system has been designed but not yet adopted — the internal team needs governance support
- An AI initiative requires ongoing decision-rights management without a dedicated transformation executive
- A consulting firm has an active healthcare engagement and needs a specialist advisor for the operational intelligence layer

### Problem
After a diagnostic or transformation engagement, organizations frequently reach the same inflection point: they have a plan, a backlog, and possibly a working system — but not the internal discipline to govern it. Without a weekly operating cadence, recovery momentum dissipates within 60 days. The question is not whether the buyer understands this. Most do. The question is whether they can justify the cost of a fractional engagement versus assuming the risk of internal execution without oversight.

### Deliverables
Monthly (adjust based on engagement scope):
1. **Weekly operating cadence** — Standing call with transformation or operations leadership: progress, blockers, priorities.
2. **Monthly operating system review** — Formal assessment of delivery progress, governance health, backlog status, and emerging risks.
3. **Executive status brief** — Monthly one-page summary for sponsor or board.
4. **Backlog and priority guidance** — Ongoing input on sequencing and scope management.
5. **AI governance guidance** — Specific input on where AI should operate, where humans must approve, and how to evolve the system as AI capabilities expand.

### Timeline
3–6 months initial commitment. Monthly renewal option.

### Pricing Range
**$12,000–$20,000 per month**

Lower end: Light-touch advisory (2–4 hours/week, primarily asynchronous).
Upper end: Substantive embedded advisory (6–8 hours/week, including structured calls and deliverable review).

Do not price below $12k/month. Below that, the engagement cannot be delivered at a level that produces measurable results, and it will not be perceived as senior-level advisory.

### Outcomes
- Transformation momentum maintained between major milestones
- AI and automation initiatives governed with clear human-approval requirements
- Executive sponsor has a trusted monthly view of program health
- Internal team develops operational discipline that reduces external dependency over time

### Sales Narrative
"The most common failure mode after a transformation engagement isn't technical. The program loses its operating cadence within 60 days. Meetings become less frequent. The backlog stops moving. The sponsor stops seeing structured updates. I operate as a fractional transformation lead — I hold the weekly cadence, govern the backlog, keep the AI governance model honest, and make sure the sponsor stays informed. It costs a fraction of a full-time hire. It produces the outcome a full-time hire would produce during the critical momentum window."

### Included
- Weekly operating cadence (standing call and async updates)
- Monthly operating system review with written summary
- Executive status brief
- Backlog and priority guidance
- AI governance design and guidance
- Async availability within agreed response window

### Excluded
- Hands-on implementation or software development
- Management of internal employees or direct reports
- Legal or regulatory advisory
- Deliverables outside agreed scope without adjustment

### Proof Available Today
- Transformation Recovery Framework (six layers: Visibility, Governance, Workflow, Decisioning, Adoption, Measurement)
- Healthcare transformation leadership experience at enterprise scale
- Operating cadence and governance model design demonstrated in prior employment engagements

### Proof Still Needed
- A completed TKO diagnostic or build engagement that converted to fractional advisory — this is the cleanest proof story
- Published case study showing transformation momentum maintained over a 3–6 month engagement
